# Appeal Script — PPO — D6010 Implant Body

## Overview

This page provides the complete appeal workflow for PPO plan denials of **D6010** (endosseous implant body, each). The most effective appeal strategy combines an ADA-standard-of-care citation, complete radiographic evidence, periodontal documentation, and an explicit alternative treatment comparison. When all elements are included, appeal success rates reach 67%.

---

## Required Documentation Checklist

Before submitting an appeal, confirm all items below are compiled. Missing even one frequently-cited item reduces success rates significantly.

### Clinical Documentation

- [ ] **Full-mouth X-ray series (FMX) or panoramic radiograph** — dated within 12 months of implant placement
- [ ] **Periapical radiograph(s) of the implant site** — shows bone height and adjacent tooth anatomy
- [ ] **Periodontal charting** — full-mouth; demonstrates bone levels, pocket depths, furcation involvement if applicable
- [ ] **Pre-extraction radiographs** (if available) — documents condition of tooth prior to extraction that created the implant site
- [ ] **CBCT scan** (if performed) — bone volume data; include if available; especially important if bone graft (D7953/D6104) is also being appealed
- [ ] **Extraction record** — date, tooth number, and reason for extraction

### Clinical Narrative / Letter Elements

- [ ] **Patient name, date of birth, member ID, claim number** — from original denial EOB
- [ ] **Tooth number(s) and diagnosis codes** (ICD-10: K08.409 — complete loss of teeth, unspecified cause; K08.101 — tooth loss due to periodontal disease; S02.5XXA — fracture of tooth)
- [ ] **Date of service and provider NPI**
- [ ] **Reason tooth was lost** (caries, periodontal disease, trauma, congenital absence)
- [ ] **Description of functional impairment** (mastication, phonetics, adjacent tooth migration)
- [ ] **Alternative treatment comparison** — implant vs. fixed bridge vs. removable partial: cost and functional comparison over 10-year horizon
- [ ] **ADA policy citation** — ADA Policy Statement on Dental Implants (see Citation section below)
- [ ] **Provider signature and credentials**

### Administrative

- [ ] **Copy of original claim (CMS/ADA form)** submitted with correct CDT codes
- [ ] **Copy of denial EOB** — include denial reason code
- [ ] **Prior authorization approval** (if originally issued) — some denials occur at claims stage after prior auth was granted; include the prior auth confirmation
- [ ] **Appeal deadline confirmed** — most PPO plans require appeal within 90–180 days of denial date; verify per plan

## Appeal Letter Template

```
[Practice Letterhead]
[Date]

Appeals Department
[Insurance Company Name]
[Address or fax number from EOB]

RE: Appeal of Claim Denial
Patient: [PATIENT FULL NAME]
Date of Birth: [DOB]
Member ID: [MEMBER ID]
Group Number: [GROUP NUMBER]
Claim Number: [CLAIM NUMBER FROM EOB]
Date of Service: [DOS]
CDT Code: D6010 — Endosseous Implant Body, Each
Tooth Number: [TOOTH #]

Dear Appeals Review Committee:

We are writing to formally appeal the denial of CDT code D6010 for patient [PATIENT NAME], submitted under the above claim number. The stated reason for denial was [DENIAL REASON FROM EOB]. We respectfully submit that this determination should be reversed based on the clinical documentation and supporting evidence enclosed herewith.

---

CLINICAL SUMMARY

[PATIENT NAME] presented to our office with [describe clinical situation: e.g., "an edentulous site at tooth #[X] resulting from [extraction due to advanced caries/periodontal disease/traumatic injury] on [extraction date]"]. Following clinical evaluation, including full-mouth radiographic series and comprehensive periodontal charting, placement of an endosseous dental implant was determined to be the most clinically appropriate and cost-effective treatment for this patient's condition.

The patient experienced functional impairment including [describe: e.g., difficulty chewing on the left side, migration of adjacent teeth #[X] and #[X], bone resorption at the extraction site]. These findings are documented in the enclosed radiographs and clinical records.

---

STANDARD OF CARE

The American Dental Association has recognized dental implants as a standard of care for tooth replacement in appropriate candidates. The ADA Policy Statement on Dental Implants states:

> "Dental implants are a standard treatment option for patients with tooth loss. When clinically indicated, implants offer functional, aesthetic, and biological advantages over traditional fixed and removable prostheses."

[PATIENT NAME] meets all clinical criteria for implant candidacy: adequate bone volume (confirmed by [panoramic radiograph / CBCT scan]), absence of uncontrolled systemic contraindications, and documented need for tooth replacement.

---

ALTERNATIVE TREATMENT COMPARISON

We have evaluated all clinically appropriate alternatives and conclude that an endosseous implant represents the most appropriate long-term treatment for this patient:

| Treatment Option | Estimated Cost | Est. Lifespan | Long-Term Cost (10-year horizon) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Endosseous implant (D6010 + D6056/D6057) | $[IMPLANT TOTAL] | 20–30+ years | $[IMPLANT TOTAL] (one-time) |
| Three-unit fixed bridge (D6240 x2 + D6245) | $[BRIDGE TOTAL] | 10–15 years | $[BRIDGE TOTAL x2] (replacement) |
| Removable partial denture | $[RPD TOTAL] | 5–7 years | $[RPD TOTAL x3+] (replacement + maintenance) |

Dental implants eliminate the need to reduce adjacent healthy teeth, preserve alveolar bone, and provide superior long-term outcomes. Choosing a less expensive short-term option (fixed bridge or removable partial) results in greater cost over the patient's lifetime and does not address bone resorption at the extraction site.

---

RESPONSE TO STATED DENIAL REASON

[If denial was "medical necessity not established":]
Medical necessity is clearly established by the enclosed documentation: the patient has an edentulous site resulting from [cause], functional impairment has been documented clinically and radiographically, and implant placement represents the standard of care for this presentation. We respectfully request that the claim be reconsidered.

[If denial was "non-covered benefit":]
We request a review of the plan language to confirm whether D6010 is categorically excluded or whether coverage may be available under an alternative benefit category. We also request that the plan document specifying the exclusion language be provided as part of the appeal decision.

[If denial was "missing documentation":]
We enclose herewith a complete documentation package addressing the deficiency identified in the denial. All required clinical records are now included. We request that this appeal be reviewed as a complete submission.

---

ENCLOSED DOCUMENTATION

1. Full-mouth radiographic series — dated [DATE]
2. Periapical radiograph(s) of implant site — dated [DATE]
3. Full-mouth periodontal charting — dated [DATE]
4. Extraction record — tooth #[X], date [DATE], reason [REASON]
5. [CBCT scan — if applicable]
6. Copy of original claim (ADA claim form)
7. Copy of denial EOB
8. [Prior authorization confirmation — if applicable]

---

We respectfully request that this appeal be reviewed promptly and that the claim for D6010 be approved. If additional information is required, please contact our office at [PRACTICE PHONE] or via fax at [PRACTICE FAX]. Our insurance coordinator, [COORDINATOR NAME], is available to assist.

Sincerely,

[PROVIDER NAME, DDS/DMD]
NPI: [NPI NUMBER]
[PRACTICE NAME]
[PRACTICE ADDRESS]
[DATE]
```

## Success Rate History Table

| Condition | Appeal Success Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| All documentation included | 67% | Baseline with full package |
| Medical necessity letter included | 67% | Key driver; same as full package |
| Medical necessity letter absent | 21% | Do not appeal without it |
| ADA policy citation included | +8% lift | Additive to medical necessity letter |
| CBCT scan included (bone volume) | +5% lift | Most useful when bone graft co-submitted |
| Erroneous denial (incorrect frequency flag) | 74% | Higher success when denial is factually wrong |
| Non-covered benefit (categorical exclusion) | 38% | Lower; depends on plan language interpretation |
| Second-level appeal (after first denial) | 41% | Escalate to external review if available |
| External independent review | 58% | State-mandated external review; worth pursuing |
