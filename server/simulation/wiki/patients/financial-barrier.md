# Financial Barrier

## Profile Summary
Patient wants treatment but cannot pay out-of-pocket. Common in: self-pay patients, underinsured, large multi-tooth cases. Key: financing friction is the #1 reason for preventable treatment decline. Apply early — don't wait for patient to ask.

## Financing Options (ranked by acceptance rate)

| Option | Approval Rate | Monthly (60mo) | Best For |
|--------|---------------|----------------|----------|
| CareCredit 60mo | 71% | $333/mo on $20k | All-on-4 patients |
| Lending Club 84mo | 64% | $267/mo on $20k | Longer-term planners |
| In-house 12mo 0% | 89% | $1,667/mo on $20k | Short-term high-income |
| Denefits (no-credit) | 91% | varies | Declined CareCredit |

## Intelligence
- Pre-qualify BEFORE presenting treatment plan — leads to 2.3× higher acceptance
- Present as "monthly investment" not "total cost"
- Always have a backup option ready if primary financing declines
- Denefits catches ~40% of CareCredit declines
