Unions are a goldmine for dental practices because they represent large, organized groups of workers who typically have dental benefits but are often underserved or confused about how to maximize them. Here's a structured approach to build those relationships effectively.
Phase 1: Identify & Prioritize Target Unions
Start with the unions that have the strongest presence in the Roseville/Sacramento corridor. Your top targets should be:
Construction & Trades — Laborers (LIUNA Local 185), Carpenters (Local 46), IBEW Local 340, Plumbers & Pipefitters Local 447. These workers have solid benefits, high dental trauma rates, and are often underserved. The construction boom in Placer County makes this especially timely.
Public Sector — SEIU Local 1000 (state workers), AFSCME, CalPERS-covered employees. Sacramento being the state capital means tens of thousands of state employees within driving distance.
Healthcare Workers — CNA/NNOC nurses, Kaiser unions. They understand clinical quality and make great referral sources once you earn their trust.
Teamsters Local 150, UFCW Local 8 (grocery/retail workers) — large membership rolls, often with Delta Dental or similar plans.
Phase 2: Build Your Value Proposition
Unions care about three things for their members: access, affordability, and accountability. Structure your pitch around these:
Access — You offer general dentistry, oral surgery, AND orthodontics under one roof (or closely coordinated). Members don't get bounced around. Emphasize same-week appointments, extended hours, and emergency availability.
Affordability — Offer union-specific pricing tiers for services not fully covered by insurance (implants, cosmetic, ortho). A transparent pricing sheet goes a long way. Your $14,995 full arch pricing is already competitive — frame that as an exclusive union member benefit.
Accountability — Offer a dedicated point of contact for union members. A single phone number or email where their members get priority scheduling and billing support. This is what separates you from every other office sending a flyer.
Phase 3: Engagement Strategy
Step 1 — Get in the Door. Contact the union's Business Agent or Benefits Coordinator directly. Don't pitch cold — lead with value. Offer a free lunch-and-learn or health fair booth. Topics that work well: "Maximizing Your Dental Benefits Before Year-End," "Workplace Dental Injuries: What You Need to Know," or "The Truth About Implants vs. Dentures."
Step 2 — Health Fairs & Job Site Visits. Construction unions especially love on-site or union hall health screenings. Bring a portable setup, do free oral cancer screenings, hand out custom branded kits. This is where trust gets built face-to-face.
Step 3 — Negotiate a Preferred Provider Agreement. This doesn't have to be a formal insurance contract. It can be a simple MOU where you agree to offer union members a specific discount schedule, waive certain fees, or provide priority access. Put it in writing so the union leadership can present it to their membership as a tangible benefit they secured.
Step 4 — Co-Branded Materials. Create flyers, digital content, and benefit guides with the union's logo alongside yours (with their permission). This signals endorsement and dramatically increases trust.
Phase 4: Retention & Expansion
Quarterly check-ins with union leadership — share utilization data (how many members you've seen, what services were provided). This helps them justify the partnership to their board.
Member testimonials — with permission, collect video or written testimonials from union members. These are powerful when presented back to the union and on your website.
Referral program — offer a small incentive (whitening kit, gift card) for union members who refer colleagues. Word of mouth within a union hall spreads fast.
Annual benefits education — offer to present at their annual meetings or open enrollment periods. Position yourself as their go-to dental resource, not just another provider.
Phase 5: Scale It
Once you have two or three union partnerships producing consistent patient flow, use those as case studies to approach the next tier. The Sacramento Central Labor Council is an umbrella organization that can introduce you to dozens of affiliated unions at once — but you want to go there with proven results, not just a pitch.
